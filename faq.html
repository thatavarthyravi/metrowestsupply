Shipping FAQ

We fulfill high-volume material needs for tenant turns and restoration work across North America. These FAQs cover delivery options, timing, receiving best practices, and issue resolution.

What delivery options do you offer?

Most programs support one or more of the following: jobsite delivery, dock delivery, local courier, will-call pickup, and drop-ship direct from wholesalers (when applicable).

What is will-call pickup?

Will-call means the order is staged at a supplier counter or warehouse for pickup by your team or courier. This is useful for urgent turns or local procurement.

What is drop-ship?

Drop-ship means a wholesaler ships directly to your property or jobsite instead of shipping to a central warehouse first. This reduces handling time for certain categories and regions.

How long does shipping take?

Timing depends on region, supplier availability, and order size. For repeat programs, we can define target SLAs by market. If an order is time-sensitive, mark it as “rush” in your request.

Do you ship to multiple properties?

Yes. We support multi-site shipments and consolidated ordering. Provide a site list and delivery windows, and we’ll coordinate split shipments and tracking.

Receiving best practices

  • Confirm delivery location (leasing office, dock, unit number, gate code).
  • Verify item counts and note visible damage on the delivery receipt.
  • Photograph damaged cartons or pallets before moving product.
  • Store liquids and temperature-sensitive items per manufacturer guidance.

What if a shipment arrives damaged or incomplete?

Report issues as soon as possible with photos and a short description. We’ll help coordinate replacement, credit, or supplier claim handling depending on the shipment method and product category.
